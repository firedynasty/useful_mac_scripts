Summary 02/19/2026 - Mic Recording AppleScript for Karabiner

GOAL:
Create a toggle AppleScript (mapped to Karabiner shortcut Cmd+Shift+7) that:
- Press once: starts recording from MacBook Air Microphone
- Press again: stops recording, converts to MP3, saves to ~/Downloads/spokenMp3Files/

USE CASE:
Record yourself speaking Chinese text (or any language) and save as MP3 for review.

FILES CREATED:
- /Users/stanleytan/desktop/admin_code/for_karabiner_record_mic_to_mp3.applescript (Karabiner version)

- /Users/stanleytan/desktop/admin_code/test_audio_record.applescript (test version with dialogs)

WHAT WORKS:
- Recording with sox directly in Terminal: NO buzz (e.g. your existing `re` or `reco` commands)
- sox -t coreaudio "MacBook Air Microphone" /tmp/test.wav  <-- clean audio
- PID file approach (/tmp/mic_recorder.pid) for toggle detection works
- WAV to MP3 conversion via ffmpeg works

ISSUE TO TROUBLESHOOT:
- Running sox (or ffmpeg) in background from AppleScript produces a periodic buzz/thump sound
- ffmpeg avfoundation: buzz
- sox via helper script with &: buzz
- sox directly in Terminal: NO buzz
- Root cause: backgrounding the audio process (via & in shell) causes audio buffer artifacts
- Latest attempt: launch sox inside a real Terminal.app window via AppleScript `do script` -- not yet tested
